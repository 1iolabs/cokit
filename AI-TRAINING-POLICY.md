This policy expresses 1io BRANDGUARDIAN GmbH's reservation of rights, to the
extent permitted by applicable law, against reliance on statutory text-and-
data-mining exceptions and automated crawling/training uses in connection with
COKIT content.

This policy is separate from the software licenses that apply to individual
components.

## Rights Reservation

1io expressly reserves, to the extent permitted by applicable law, its rights
under **Article 4(3) of Directive (EU) 2019/790** (DSM Directive), as
transposed into German law by **Section 44b UrhG**.

This reservation is directed at:

- AGPL-licensed COKIT source code, documentation, examples and comments;
- source-available GUARD components (distributed separately under the
  1io Guard Source License CGSL-1.0);
- repository documentation, metadata, configuration and website content;
- automated crawling and ingestion of the above for AI/ML model training,
  fine-tuning, embedding, or indexing workflows that rely on statutory TDM
  exceptions.

## Important Limitation

Nothing in this policy is intended to narrow permissions that are expressly
granted under the applicable software license for a specific component.

In particular, for components licensed under **AGPL-3.0-only**, the software
license remains the primary source of granted rights. This policy addresses
only uses that go beyond the scope of the software license, such as training
AI/ML models on the content.

## Uses Requiring Written Permission

To the extent permitted by applicable law, written permission is required for:

1. using covered repository or website content as training data for AI/ML
   models;
2. fine-tuning or preference-optimization on covered repository or website
   content;
3. systematic crawling/scraping of covered content for AI/ML dataset creation;
4. building RAG / embedding / indexing systems over covered documentation or
   source content beyond ordinary developer browsing and project maintenance.

## Legal Basis

- **DSM Directive, Article 4(3):** allows rightholders to reserve rights
  against text and data mining by appropriate means, including
  machine-readable means for online content.
- **German law, Section 44b UrhG:** implements the same mechanism.
- **AI Act, Article 53(1)(c):** requires providers of general-purpose AI
  models to identify and comply with such reservations.
